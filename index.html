Rising Shelter Backlog: The Toll of Irresponsible Pet Ownership

Pet culture has developed rapidly since ancient times, with dogs being one of the most popular pets due to their loyalty and faithfulness. In modern times, dogs are still deeply embedded in human lives as a representation of companionship as well as beloved members of the family. In recent years, the number of dog owners has grown sustainably. In the United States, the percentage of households owning dogs has increased from 38.4% to 44.5% from 2016 to 2022[1].

However, as the number of dog owners has snowballed, the number of dogs abandoned or relinquished also experienced a huge increase, which results in great pressure on animal shelters in the U.S. According to Stephanie Filer, Shelter Animals Count’s executive director, “Shelters are quite literally at crisis[2].” The below graph shows the gap between the dogs entering and leaving animal shelters, the structure of entry and outcome types of these dogs, and how they have changed in recent years.

From 2020 to 2023, the gap keeps increasing, taking lots of the shelter's resources. As a result, most of the dogs backlogged in shelters will only see euthanasia as their end. The non-live outcome of dogs in shelters has indeed seen an obvious increase over the years. The root of all this is the irresponsible abandonment of their dogs by dog owners, as stray dogs and relinquished dogs take up the majority of total dogs entering animal shelters. As reported in the article on TIME[2], the biggest reasons that dog owners give up their dogs are landlord disputes, pet restrictions, and financial pressure.

Economic Consideration

Make a mature decision


Owning a pet dog requires a lot of financial and community support - we all know this. The problem is that many pet owners don't think deeply enough about the decision to get a dog. A survey conducted on 2,000 dog owners showed that 54% of dog owners have regretted the decision to keep a dog[3]. It is these immature dog ownership decisions that are contributing to the problem of the shelter backlog and the threat of death to the dogs in the shelters. Finance is one of the biggest burdens for dog owners. Study shows that in 2023, 91% of dog owners have suffered from financial stress due to pet care costs[4]. Thus, we develpoed the following visualization to show an overview of dog keeping cost and help dog owners make more informed decisions about their pet ownership based on their financial status.

Our visualization breaks down the annual spending on dogs and cats, revealing insights into the costs associated with pet ownership. Explore the contributions of food, veterinary care, toys, grooming, and other expenses to understand the financial commitment of caring for your furry companions. Especially if you already have a cute kitten, consider whether you can afford a new dog.

Besides, discover the affordability of owning a dog in different cities across the country. Our visualization compares the median household income with the cost of owning a dog, providing insights into the financial implications of pet ownership in various locations[1].

Dog Breed Selection

Our interactive scatter plot and radar plots provide valuable insights into the world of dog breeds, helping prospective dog owners make informed decisions. The scatter plot showcases the popularity of different dog breeds alongside their overall scores, offering a quick glance at which breeds are most favored. Click on any point to reveal detailed characteristics of that breed in the radar plot on the right. This dynamic feature allows you to explore the traits and attributes of each breed, aiding in your decision-making process.

Additionally, our search functionality enables you to quickly find specific breeds, making it easier to compare and evaluate different options. Whether you're looking for a highly popular breed or a hidden gem, our visualizations provide a comprehensive overview to guide your decision. Explore the scatter plot and radar plots to discover the perfect furry companion for your lifestyle and preferences.

Tailoring your search for the perfect canine companion has never been easier with our customizable scatter plot. By adjusting the weights of key characteristics such as average height, average weight, intelligence, lifetime cost, longevity, and obedience, you can create a personalized score that reflects your priorities in a pet.

As you tweak these sliders, the scatter plot dynamically updates to display each breed's score based on your preferences. This allows you to explore how different breeds rank according to your chosen criteria, helping you find a dog that aligns with your lifestyle and preferences.

Shelters & Dog-Friendly Rentals

Are you considering adopting a dog but unsure about the availability of shelters or dog-friendly rentals in your area? Our interactive visualization provides a comprehensive overview to help you make an informed decision. Explore the number of shelters, non-kill shelters, and dog-friendly rentals across different states in the US. Use the map selector to switch between different categories and discover which states offer the most options for shelter and rental accommodations.

The first map displays the total number of shelters in each state, with a stacked bar chart showing the top states with the highest number of shelters. This chart also includes the number of non-kill shelters in each of these top states, giving you an insight into the availability of shelters and the commitment to animal welfare.

Switching to the non-kill shelters map, you can see the distribution of non-kill shelters across the US. The accompanying stacked bar chart highlights the top states with the highest number of non-kill shelters, providing valuable information about the states' efforts in promoting animal welfare and reducing euthanasia rates.

For those looking for dog-friendly rentals, the third and fourth map displays the number of such rentals in each state. The stacked bar chart complements this map by showing the states with the highest number of small dog-friendly rentals or big dog-friendly rentals, helping you find the most suitable accommodation for your furry friend.
